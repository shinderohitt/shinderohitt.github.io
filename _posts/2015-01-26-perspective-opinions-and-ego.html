I always try to stay away from arguments. Most, most people don't change their views about things based on other's opinions. Instead what they do is -- try to prove themselves right, and in the process waste their energy for nothing. I see no point in it.

Each person's eyes have an invisible filtering goggles on it. This invisible frame has a set of rules in it which filters out, or bends the rays of light and sends it to their retina. These set of rules are different for each. And they are developed over time, in their course of lives. Every single day brings new experiences, new learnings. With which their brains keep re-evaluating these rules. Sometimes old entries are removed or improved, sometimes new ones are added. And so, everybody sees the same picture differently. That is perspective. Once you realize this, you start respecting, and ignoring others' opinions. Which is really important.

However, it is not always possible to tolerate conflict in opinions. Sometimes you just have no other way but to face it. When it happens, you should rely on your past experiences with the person, and communicate accordingly. Remember -- Respect. If your colleague has a different opinion, that does not mean that person has committed some crime. It just means that they are seeing the processed picture - which is, different than yours. What you need to take care of, most importantly, is your tone. Out of excitement, thinking how wrong the other person is, and trying to show the the right path, we talk in a loud, high pitched voice. Which pisses an average Joe, and that is exactly when these arguments take a way different path. It brings in Ego. When it comes in, nobody wants to lose.

In my school years, I was so fascinated by space science. In grade 9, someone had gifted me this book which had -- all these maps of the sky set to 9PM, for each month. Every night I would go out, and cross check if each star existed the way it was shown in the map. With the map, there was so much more information given about each thing that appeared in it (mostly stars). What fascinated me was the idea of how tiny, tiny we are.

Considering the age of our surroundings, we are a very, very young species on Earth, and we really know nothing. Dinosaurs went extinct because of an impact of an Asteroid with the Earth. If we are to believe that, there is a possibility of that happening again. Imagine space. I don't want to depress you, but trying to make you look at the bigger picture. Forget about your worries, and mundane tasks for a moment. Give it some rest, and think. Really. How many of your ancestors you remember? Look back in past. Think. Think hard. 2? 3? 4? .. 10? Probably not. Now look ahead. Based on this little experiment, how many generations down the path will be able to remember you ever dwelled on Earth? A hard question, really. But you get the idea.

Let's get back to the topic. Ego. If You, Yourself are not going to live forever, four generations down the line you will be forgotten, then WHY?
